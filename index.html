SACE (South Australian Certificate of Education) is a qualification that students in South Australia work towards in Years 11 and 12. It helps them gain the skills and knowledge needed for further study, work, or training. SACE includes subjects in areas like English, Maths, Science, and the Arts. Students must complete a certain number of credits, including compulsory subjects like the Research Project. SACE encourages independent learning and problem-solving, helping students get ready for life after school.
